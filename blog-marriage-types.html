What Are the 30 Different Types of Marriage?

Published on December 26, 2024 • By EWU Matrimony Team

Marriage is a universal institution, yet its forms vary wildly across cultures, history, and sociology. While most of us are familiar with monogamy, sociologists have classified marriage into numerous categories based on spouse number, partner selection, and cultural rules. Here is a comprehensive look at the diverse landscape of matrimonial unions.

Based on Number of Spouses

  • Monogamy: Marriage between one man and one woman. The most common global norm.
  • Serial Monogamy: Frequent remarriage; having several spouses sequentially, but only one at a time.
  • Polygamy: Marriage to more than one spouse at a time.
  • Polygyny: One man marrying multiple women.
  • Sororal Polygyny: A man marrying sisters.
  • Non-sororal Polygyny: A man marrying multiple women who are not related.
  • Polyandry: One woman marrying multiple men.
  • Fraternal Polyandry: A woman marrying a group of brothers.
  • Non-fraternal Polyandry: A woman marrying multiple men who are strangers to each other.
  • Group Marriage (Cenogamy): A group of men married to a group of women.

Based on Selection Rules (Endogamy & Exogamy)

  • Endogamy: Marrying within one's own specific social group or caste.
  • Tribal Endogamy: Marrying within the tribe.
  • Caste Endogamy: Marrying within the caste.
  • Exogamy: Marrying outside one's own group.
  • Gotra Exogamy: Prohibiting marriage within the same clan/lineage (Gotra).
  • Village Exogamy: Prohibiting marriage within the same village.

Based on Lineage and Kinship

  • Levirate: A widow marrying her deceased husband's brother.
  • Sororate: A widower marrying his deceased wife's sister.
  • Cross-Cousin Marriage: Marriage between children of siblings of the opposite sex (e.g., father's sister's child).
  • Parallel-Cousin Marriage: Marriage between children of siblings of the same sex (e.g., father's brother's child).
  • Hypergamy (Anuloma): Marrying a partner of higher social status/caste.
  • Hypogamy (Pratiloma): Marrying a partner of lower social status/caste.

Other Forms

  • Love Marriage: Based on mutual affection and choice.
  • Arranged Marriage: Partners selected by family/elders.
  • Civil Marriage: Registered by the state/government.
  • Religious Marriage: Solemnized by religious authority.
  • Common-Law Marriage: Cohabitation recognized as marriage without formal ceremony.
  • Ghost Marriage: Marrying a deceased person to continue lineage (historical practice).
  • Fixed-Term Marriage: Temporary contract marriage.
