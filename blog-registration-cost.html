What Is the Cost of Marriage Registration in Bangladesh?

Published on December 26, 2024 • By EWU Matrimony Team

Planning a wedding involves many details, and one of the most important legal steps in Bangladesh is marriage registration. Understanding the costs associated with the "Kabinnama" (Marriage Deed) is essential for proper budgeting and legal compliance. As of 2024, the government has updated specific fees based on the Dower money (Mohorana).

Government Registration Fees (2024 Update)

The Ministry of Law, Justice and Parliamentary Affairs has set clear guidelines for Muslim marriage registration fees. These fees are primarily calculated based on the varying amounts of Dower (Mohorana) agreed upon by the parties.

Dower Amount (Mohorana) Registration Fee
Up to BDT 4,00,000 (4 Lac) BDT 12.50 per BDT 1,000 (BDT 1,250 per Lac)
Recent Amendment Update BDT 14 per BDT 1,000 (BDT 1,400 per Lac) for first 5 Lac
Above BDT 5,00,000 BDT 100 for every additional BDT 1,00,000 (1 Lac)

Detailed Breakdown

  • Up to 5 Lac Taka: The fee is BDT 14 for every BDT 1,000. For example, if your Mohorana is BDT 5,00,000, the fee will be BDT 7,000.
  • Above 5 Lac Taka: For any amount exceeding 5 Lac, the fee is significantly lower—only BDT 100 for every subsequent Lac. For instance, if the Mohorana is BDT 10,00,000, the fee would be BDT 7,000 (for the first 5 Lac) + BDT 500 (for the next 5 Lac) = BDT 7,500 total.
  • Minimum Fee: Regardless of the Mohorana amount, the minimum registration fee is fixed at BDT 200.

Additional Costs

Besides the government registration fee, there may be other small charges depending on your location or specific services:

  • Kabinnama Copy: To obtain a certified copy of the Kabinnama, a fee (typically around BDT 50) is charged.
  • Commission/Travel: If the Marriage Registrar (Kazi) is invited to a private venue (like a home or community center) to conduct the marriage, reasonable travel allowances or commission fees are often paid, though these are not strict government levies.
  • Divorce Registration: The fee for registering a divorce is fixed at BDT 500.

Why Registration is Mandatory?

Under the Muslim Marriages and Divorces (Registration) Act, 1974, registering a marriage is mandatory. It ensures legal protection for both spouses, securing rights related to dower, maintenance, inheritance, and the legitimacy of children. An unregistered marriage can lead to significant legal complications in the future.
